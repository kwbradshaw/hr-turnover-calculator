Transparency

Methodology & sources

We believe calculators should be honest about what's a hard research finding vs. a configurable assumption. Here's exactly what underpins each number.

Cost Item Formula Source / Attribution
Lost productivity Weekly total comp × weeks vacant × vacancy loss % Configurable assumption (default 70%). Accounts for partial team absorption.
Team coverage Weekly total comp × coverage % × weeks vacant Uses total comp (not salary) to include employer benefit costs per BLS guidance.
Recruiting expenses Direct dollar input User-entered. Covers agency fees, ads, background checks, referral bonuses.
Interview time Interview hours × avg interviewer hourly rate Includes recruiter screens, panel interviews, and debrief sessions.
Onboarding overhead Weekly total comp × ramp weeks × onboarding % Configurable estimate (default 15%). Not a specific SHRM benchmark.
Reduced ramp output Weekly total comp × ramp weeks × productivity gap % Configurable assumption (default 40% gap). Common HR consulting figure; not attributed to a single study.
Benefits rate default 25% of base salary U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics — Employer Costs for Employee Compensation (BLS avg ~30%; 25% is conservative).
Turnover cost range 50–200%+ of annual salary Center for American Progress; Work Institute 2023 Retention Report.

Assuming 100% of productivity disappears overstates the cost, since coworkers often absorb some work and some projects are delayed rather than lost. A 70% default is more defensible. When an employee leaves, their work gets redistributed. Team coverage estimates the value of that extra labor — via overtime, contractor fees, or burning down your team's available capacity.
