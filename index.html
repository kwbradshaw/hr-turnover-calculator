How it works

Six cost categories, one real number

Our model adds up every cost that rarely appears in a single budget line.

01
📉

Lost productivity

The salary and benefits value of work not produced while the seat is empty, week by week.

02
🧑‍🤝‍🧑

Team coverage

What existing employees earn (overtime or otherwise) to absorb the departed employee's workload.

03
📢

Recruiting costs

Agency fees, job board postings, background checks, and any referral bonuses paid to fill the role.

04
🗓️

Interview time

Every interviewer hour has a salary cost. For senior hires, this alone can reach $10,000+.

05
📚

Onboarding & training

HR and manager hours spent onboarding, plus access provisioning, equipment, and orientation programs.

06
📈

Reduced ramp output

A new hire typically operates at 60% capacity for their first 3 months. That gap has a dollar value.

FAQ

The salary savings are immediately offset by recruiting costs, team overtime, and lost output. The bigger hidden cost is the "ramp gap" — a new hire operates at 60–70% productivity for their first 2–3 months, which means you're effectively paying full salary for part-time output during that window.
When an employee leaves, their work doesn't disappear — it gets redistributed. Team coverage accounts for overtime pay, temporary contractor fees, or the cost of burning down your existing team's capacity to absorb the extra workload while the seat is open.
The model is based on SHRM, Gallup, and Center for American Progress methodology. It accounts for the six largest quantifiable cost categories. It does not account for morale impact on remaining staff, institutional knowledge loss, or customer relationship disruption — which means the real number is often even higher.
